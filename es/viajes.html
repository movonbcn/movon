Park Güell - Sagrada Familia - Torre Agbar - La Pedrera

Park Güell

Main work of Gaudí in Barcelona. Is one of the most beautiful icons of the city. Is located in the upper part of Barcelona, wich has wonderful views of the city

Torre Agbar

Curently called “La Torre Glòries” is a skyscraper tower located near “Plaça de les Glòries Catalanes”, wich marks the gateway to the new technological district of Barcelona.

Sagrada Familia

Is a catholic basilica of Barcelona, designed by the architect Antoni Gaudí. It’s the most visited church in Europe.

La Pedrera

Is a modernist building in Barcelona designed by the archiquetc Antoni Gaudí, was built between 1906 and 1910. It’s one of the 10 most visited places in Barcelona. It’s a reflection of the artistic fullness of Gaudí.

Casa Batlló - Arc del Triomf - Parc de la Ciutadella
Barri Gótic - Passeig Marítim - Mirador Colón

Casa Batlló

It’s a building designed by the architect Antoni Gaudí. It’s a complete remodeling of a previously exiting building on the site, work of Emilio Sala Cortés.

Arc del Triomf

The monument is classical in shape and proportions and features ground-Breaking sculptural and decorative finishes replete with symbolism. It has become one of the city’s iconic landmarks.

Parc de la Ciutadella

Built towards the end of the end of the 19th century, this was the first place to be specifically designed as a public park. It contains over a hundred species of plants, with a large number of century-old trees and interesting sculptures.

Barri Gótic

It’s the oldest nucleus of the city and its historic center. The Roman Cardus and Decumanus are the historical urbanization axes of the neigborhood at it’s highest part, the old Mount Táber.

Passeig Marítim

Barcelona’s seafront promenade is one of the most pleasant areas of the city to go for stroll. It has a 5 kilómeters length it’s a very pleasant walk that goes near the coast.

Mirador de Colón

The Mirador de Colón, also known as the Columbus Monument, is an impressive pillar dedicated to the explorer Chistopher Columbus in Barcelona with an observation deck at the top.

Parc de Montjüic

Montjüic is one of Barcelona’s green lungs and best vantage points. This little world os history, gardens, museums, cultural centres and Olympic facilities makes it unique.

Estadi Olímpic

Opened on May 20, 1929 and rebuilt years later for the Olympic Games -Barcelona 1992. It was the venue for the opening and closing ceremonies of the XXV Olympic Games of 1992 and the athletics events.

Pueblo Español

Designed for the 1929 Barcelona International Exposition, the “Pueblo Español” is a charming outdoors museum, accommodating one of the most beautiful architecture and villages in Spain.

MNAC

Is the national museum of Catalan. The museum exhibits a collection of Romanesque paintings and Catalan art from the 19th and 20th century. Was constructed for the International Expo in 1929 and is located in the Palau Nacional building of Montjüic.

Font Màgica

One of the most famous attractions of Barcelona is the spectacle of water, music and colorful lights that the magical fountain of Montjüic offers us. This was designed by Carles Buïgas for the great International Exhibition of 1929.

Barcelona Aquarium

Visit the ocean and the seas of the world in this spectacular aquarium.

Palau de la música

A modernist building built in 1905, where you can contemplate its interior through a guided tour inside.

La Boquería

It’s a municipal market located on “La Rambla de Barcelona”. Besides being a place where you can buy all kinds of fresh products, it’s also a tourist attraction
